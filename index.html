Info about F1 Cars

Formula One cars are the fastest circuit-racing cars in the world, built for extreme speed and precision. They use hybrid power units combining a turbocharged V6 engine with electric energy recovery systems. Aerodynamics play a huge role, with wings and body design creating downforce to keep cars glued to the track. Each car is custom-designed by its team, following strict FIA regulations for safety and fairness. Modern F1 cars can exceed 350 km/h and accelerate from 0–100 km/h in about 2.5 seconds.

⚙️ First F1 Race

The first official Formula One race was held on 13 May 1950 at Silverstone Circuit in the United Kingdom. It was called the 1950 British Grand Prix and marked the start of the F1 World Championship. The race was won by Giuseppe Farina, who later became the first F1 world champion.

🌟 The Raing star of F1

Max Verstappen is a Dutch racing driver and one of the top stars in Formula One. He was born on 30 September 1997 and is the son of former F1 driver Jos Verstappen. Max made history in 2016 by becoming the **youngest race winner** in F1 at just 18 years old with Red Bull Racing. He is known for his aggressive driving style, consistency, and exceptional racecraft. Verstappen has won multiple World Championships and is considered one of the most dominant drivers of the modern era.

Sensational Rookie

Kimi Antonelli (full name Andrea Kimi Antonelli) is a young Italian driver in Formula One. He was born on 25 August 2006 and is considered one of the brightest talents from the Mercedes junior program. Antonelli quickly rose through junior categories, winning multiple titles in karting and Formula racing. He made his move toward F1 at a very young age, showing exceptional speed and maturity. Many see him as a future star and potential world champion in the making.

💾 F1 Point System

The points system in Formula One

The points system in Formula One is used to decide both the Driver’s Championship and the Constructor’s Championship.

🏁 Race Points (Top 10 finishers)

  • 1st place – 25 points
  • 2nd place – 18 points
  • 3rd place – 15 points
  • 4th place – 12 points
  • 5th place – 10 points
  • 6th place – 8 points
  • 7th place – 6 points
  • 8th place – 4 points
  • 9th place – 2 points
  • 10th place – 1 point

⚡ Bonus Point

  • 1 extra point is awarded for the fastest lap, but only if the driver finishes in the top 10

🏎️ Sprint Races

  • Short races where top 8 drivers earn points:
    • 1st – 8 points
    • 2nd – 7 points
    • 3rd – 6 points
    • 4th – 5 points
    • 5th – 4 points
    • 6th – 3 points
    • 7th – 2 points
    • 8th – 1 point

👉 Points from every race are added through the season to determine the champions.
